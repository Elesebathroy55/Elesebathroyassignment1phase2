ERNAKULAM

Ernakulam is a district of Kerala, India, that takes its name from the eponymous city division in Kochi. It is situated in the central part of the state, spans an area of about 3,000 square kilometres (1,200 sq mi), and is home to over 9% of Kerala's population. Its headquarters are located at Kakkanad. The district includes Kochi, also known as the IT and commercial capital of Kerala, which is famous for its ancient Hindu temples, churches, and mosques. The Ernakulam district covers an area of 3,068 km2 (1,185 sq mi) on the Western Coastal Plains of India. It is surrounded by the Thrissur District to the north, the Idukki District to the east, Alappuzha and Kottayam districts to the south, the Coimbatore District of Tamil Nadu to the north-east and the Laccadive Sea to the west. The state border between Ernakulam district and Coimbatore district of Tamil Nadu is not accesible by road, and is located in the entirely in forest. The district is divided geographically into highland, midland, and coastal area. The district has a moderate climate and mostly falls within the Malabar Coast moist forests ecoregion, while the highlands are part of the South Western Ghats moist deciduous forests ecoregion. Some parts of Pooyamkutty forest have Sholas, but these parts are inaccessible by road. Many types of sands, soil, and rocks are abundant here.
